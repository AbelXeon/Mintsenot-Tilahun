About Me

Hello! My name is Mintsenot Tilahun, and I am a highly motivated 3rd-year Computer Science student pursuing my degree at Infolink University College at the Hawassa Campus in Ethiopia. Ever since my introduction to computing, I have been fascinated by how structured logic and database algorithms can solve real-world system inefficiencies. For me, computer science is more than just an academic major—it is a creative medium where rigorous architecture and visual accessibility merge to solve complex problems.

Living and pursuing my education in Hawassa, a region actively experiencing digital growth across commercial and administrative sectors, gives me direct motivation. Throughout my time at Infolink, I have focused heavily on mastering programming paradigms, systems analysis, and modern database structures. I continuously challenge myself by designing structured scripts, collaborating on technical group projects, and exploring system security and logic efficiency.

I strongly believe in writing readable, maintainable code. My primary areas of study cover developing robust database solutions with robust query logic, exploring software engineering workflows, and structuring application front-ends. As I progress through my academic path, I am excited to collaborate on new digital tools, participate in software internships, and contribute to the rapidly expanding tech space in Ethiopia.
